Reflection

Reflection 1: Set Theory and Function

Through the assignments and lectures on Set Theory and Function, I gained a deeper understanding of how sets and functions are fundamental to mathematics and computer science. The assignments helped me apply theoretical concepts to solve practical problems, such as understanding relationships between sets and mapping elements using functions. Teaching from daily lectures provided clarity and ensured I could confidently complete the tasks.

Reflection 2: Recurrence Relation, Counting Method, and Pigeonhole Principle

Learning about Recurrence Relations, Counting Methods, and the Pigeonhole Principle through assignments and lectures was both challenging and rewarding. My lecturer,Mdm Nor Erne Nazira provided step-by-step explanations, which helped me understand how to break down complex problems into smaller, manageable parts. The assignments reinforced these concepts by requiring me to apply them in real-world scenarios, such as solving counting problems and analyzing recursive sequences.

Reflection 3: Discrete Probability Theory

The topic of Discrete Probability Theory introduced me to the mathematical foundation of probability in discrete systems. Through the assignments, I learned how to calculate probabilities for events, understand conditional probability, and apply these concepts to solve problems. Mdm Erne provided clear examples and explanations, which helped me build confidence in applying probability theory to practical situations.

Reflection 4: Trees and Finite Automata

Studying Trees and Finite Automata was an eye-opening experience. The assignments allowed me to explore the structure and properties of trees, such as traversal methods and their applications in algorithms. Additionally, learning about finite automata helped me understand how machines process input and recognize patterns. The lecture classes provided a strong theoretical foundation, which made it easier to approach and complete the assignments effectively.
